Best Beach Vacation Spots

Beach Vacation

Taking a beach vacation is such a great experience for the whole family. There are so many great places to go, but no matter where you go make sure you have a great time. There is nothing like watching the waves crash on the sand or rocks, while sitting on the beach in the beautiful sun. There are so many attractions to see and learn about as well.


Hawaii

Avg. Temp: Summer-85°F Winter-78°F

Hawaii consist of eight main islands: Niihau, Kauai, Oahu, Maui, Molokai, Lanai, Kahoolawe and the big island of Hawaii. At 800,000 years, the Big Island is the youngest of the island chains; however, it was the first island discovered by voyaging polunesians. The island is the worldwide leader in harvesting macadamia nuts and cords. The island houses the worlds biggest telescope and more scientific observatories in one place than anywhere else in the world. It's islands are renowned for their rugged landscapes of cliffs, waterfalls, tropical foliage and beaches with gold, red, black and even green sands.

Bahamas

Avg. Temp: Summer-83°F Winter-75°F

The Bahamas is a coral-based archipelago in the Atlantic Ocean. It has more than 700 islands and cays that range from uninhabited to packed with resorts. The Grand Bahama and Paradise Island have many hotels that are among the best. Scuba diving and snorkeling sites include the massive Andros Barrier Reef, Thunderball Grotto (which was used in the James Bond films) and the black-coral gardens off Bimini. The Bahamas is the site of Columbus' first landfall in the New World in 1492. At that time the islands were inhabited by the Lucayan, a branch of the Arawakan-speaking Taino people. .

Fiji

Avg. Temp: Summer-73°F Winter-79°F

Fiji, a country in the South Pacific, is an archipelago of more than 300 islands, of those 110 are permanently inhabited and more than 500 islets, amounting to a total land area of about 18,300 sq. km. Fiji has one of the most developed economies in the Pacific do to an abundane of forest, mineral, and fish rescources. Today, the main sources of foreign exchange are its tourist industry and sugar exports. The majority of Fiji's islands were formed through volcanic activity starting around 150 million years ago. It is famous for rugged landscapes, palm-lined beaches and coral reefs with clear lagoons. The Figi Museum, in the Victorian era Thurston Gardens, has ethnographic exhibits..
